Introduction

In reservoir engineering, identification of drive mechanism is essential. The drive mechanism provides the pressure support that the reservoir needs when production starts in order to prevent collapse. Moreover, it is preliminary feature that is required to anticipate the volume of gas/oil initial in place. Therefore, to know how much gas/oil will be recovered from the reservoir, the drive mechanism must be identified.
For gas reservoirs, the identification of the drive mechanism is graphical. Havelan & Odeh Material Balance Equation (H&O MBE) is the base that governs the production and composition data so that it generates a set of data points that will be plotted eventually. The regression approach, hence, is utilized to formulate the best-fitted curve. Then, using H&O Reference Graph, the drive mechanism can be identified to be Volumetric Drive, Water Drive, or Combination Drive of both with each having its elative strength to support the reservoir.

Mathematical Model

The H&O MBE is given by the following equation,
G (Bg - Bgi) + We = Gp Bg + Wp Bw
  • G: Volume of Initial Gas in Place (SCF).
  • Gp: Volume of Cumulative Gas Production (SCF).
  • Bg: Gas Formation Volume Factor (CF/SCF).
  • Bgi: Initial Gas Formation Volume Factor (CF/SCF).
  • We: Volume of Water Encroachment (bbl).
  • Wp: Volume of Water Production (STB).
  • Bw: Water Formation Volume Factor (bbl/STB).
The equation is simplified to,
G Eg + We = F where F = Gp Bg + Wp Bw, and Eg = Bg - Bgi
  • F: Volume of Total Gas and Water Production (CF).
  • Eg: Gas Expansion Factor (CF/SCF).
